A Carpenter and Rational Numbers

In order for a carpenter to complete a task, he or she will need the essential tools. One such tool, the measuring tape, provides the carpenter with important information related to length, width and height of objects.

Of course, in order to translate the information into useful data, the carpenter must be able to work with rational numbers.

For example

A piece of wood that is 8 feet in length must be cut into smaller pieces that measure 15 ¼ inches each.

  • How many pieces of this length can be obtained from the 8 feet?

Solution

  • 8 feet equals 96 inches
  • \[ 15 {1 \over 4} = {61 \over 4} \] (The mixed fractions turns into improper fraction)
  • \[ 96 \div {61 \over 4} = {384 \over 61} \]
  • \[ {384 \over 61} \approx 6.29in \]
  • We can obtain approximately 6.29 pieces.

SUMMARY

In this lesson, you have covered:

The product or quotient of two numbers is:

POSITIVE

\[ a \times b = ab \]

\[ - a \times (- b) = ab \]

\[ a \div b = {a \over b} \]

\[ - a \div (-b) = {a \over b} \]

NEGATIVE

\[ a \times (-b) = -ab \]

\[ - a \times -b = -ab \]

\[ a \div (-b) = -{a \over b} \]

\[ - a \div b = -{a \over b} \]
