Salamander

(n) An amphibian similar to a lizard but without scales and covered with a soft moist skin and breath by gills in the larval stage; a portable stove, a cooking device with an overhead heat source

Canteen

(n) A flask for carrying liquids; a bar at a military post or camp; a general store at a military post; an institution that serves as an informal social club; a small cafeteria or snack bar; a portable chest with compartments for carrying bottles or eating utensils

Anemic

(adj) Relating to or affected with anemia; pale and not looking well; lacking force, vitality, or spirit; deficient interest or savor; deficient in substance or quantity

Holler

(v) To cry out, as to attract attention or in pain; scream; gripe; complain

Naphtha

(n) A clear colorless flammable mixture of light hydrocarbons used chiefly as solvents and diluents

Sire

(n) Father; male ancestor; a man of position or authority (i.e. lord); an elderly man; (v) to father offspring, especially animals

Scuff

(v) To walk without lifting the feet, shuffle; to poke or shuffle a foot in exploration or embarrassment; to become scratched, chipped, or roughened by wear; to scrape (the feet) along a surface while walking or back and forth while standing; to jab at with the toe; to scratch, gouge, or wear away the surface of

Insipid

(adj) Lacking flavor; dull; not at all stimulating

Extraneous

(adj) Inessential; not constituting a vital part

Coerce

(v) To force by using pressure, intimidation, or threats
