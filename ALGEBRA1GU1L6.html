Problem Solving: Estimation

The Big Dig

Boston's expressway tunnel was budgeted at 2.5 billion dollars. Due to water seal damage, it wound up costing an estimated fifteen billion dollars.

That is six times what was estimated! It was necessary to recalculate and create a new budget for the remaining timeline.

Estimation played a vital role in completing the project. Can you think of other ways in which estimation plays a key role in real life?

Estimation Strategies

There are several estimation strategies you can use to solve word problems. Take a look at the following word problem.

Example

Robin needs supplies to stock up before a huge hurricane comes ashore. The lines at Home Depot are extremely long. She only has cash, and she wants to make sure she has enough money to cover the cost, not to mention losing valuable time should she have to go back to the bank to get more money.

Her cart has 3 plywood panels, each at $11.95, 6 gallons of water at $2.59 each, 4 battery packs at $1.79 each, 1 propane gas tank at $16.28, and 2 flash lights at $2.42 each. She has $75. Does she have enough money?

Strategy 1: Rounding to a designated place value

First, mentally round each item to the nearest dollar. Then, round to the nearest ten dollars for a quicker and easier calculation.

Strategy 3: Benchmark Numbers

Example

The following diagram shows a wall that has been partly painted. It took 3/5 of a gallon to paint this part of the wall. About how much more paint would it take to finish painting the wall?

About 1/4 of the wall is painted, according to our visual benchmark reference. A calculated estimate using the benchmark reference indicates that 3/4 is not painted. Using this estimate, we understand that we will need three times the amount of paint for the remaining 3/4 of the wall as we needed to paint 1/4 of the wall. So, 3× 3/5 gallon = 9/5 gallons =1 and 4/5 =gallons to finish the wall

SUMMARY

Check Your Estimates

Estimation is an educated guess that amounts to a reasonable answer.

  • Various strategies, such as rounding, front-end estimation, and using benchmark numbers as a reference, can be used to estimate.
  • An estimate is not an exact answer, but instead gives you a reasonable answer and a quicker method for calculation.
