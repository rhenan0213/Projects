House on Mango Street

Part 1 Vocabulary

Pillar

(n) A vertical column; a usually ornamental column or shaft, one standing alone for a monument; mainstay of an organization; a fundamental principle; a solid mass of coal, rock, or ore left standing to support a mine roof.

Brazer

(n) The tool used to solder metals with a nonferrous alloy having a lower melting point than the metal being joined.

Ferocious

(adj) Fierce or savage; extremely intense.

Hollyhocks

(n) A tall widely grown biennial or perennial herb of the mallow family that has big, rough, round leaves and tall spikes of flashy flowers; probably of Asian origin.

Comadres

(n) From the Spanish language. Godmother of one's child; mother of one's godchild; a neighbor, a female friend.

Twangy

(adj) Callous quick ringing sounds like that of a plucked banjo string; nasal speech or resonance; describing the typical speech of a region, locality, or group of people.

Hibiscus

(n) Any of a large genus (Hibiscus) of herbs, shrubs, or small trees of the mallow family with large, showy flowers and often dentate leaves.

Jaundiced

(adj) Prejudiced; hostile; cynical; having a medical condition in which there is yellow skin.

Meticulous

(adj) Extremely, oftentimes excessively, careful about little details; precise.

Heresy

(n) The crime of holding a belief that contradicts established doctrine.

    Hibiscus Jaundiced
