As you can see, all these different fields of study within biology have valuable information to contribute to our understanding of the world. Biology can mean many different things to different people. What will it mean to you?

BLOOD CELLS

Since all living things are made of cells, it makes sense that figuring out how cells work is an important task. The cell’s life cycle and reproduction are pretty crucial knowledge for studying cancer. The role of different types of nerve cells in the function of the brain could be the key to Alzheimer’s.

CHAMELEON

Evolution is the study of change over time. Much of this includes studying the ways that organisms are well-suited for their particular environment. Evolution is the common thread for all living things. Natural selection is one of the most powerful forces of nature. A topic you might study in evolution is why an antibiotic stops working over time.

PATHOLOGY

Pathology is the study of diseases and how they spread. This is very important in medicine and agriculture. You might find a pathologist studying HIV, malaria, or a fungus that attacks crops.

CORAL

In ecology, the key word is "interaction" – how organisms interact with each other and how they interact with the environment around them. An ecologist could be found studying things like how big the human population is and how many people the earth can really hold, or how a pollutant can affect the members of a certain ecosystem.

DNA STRAND

Genetics is one of the hottest fields in biology today. The entire genetic code for humans has been mapped, and now we are learning what we can do with it. The future of medicine may rest upon our ability to figure out the genetic code.

HEARTBEAT

Physiology illustrates the connections between the structure of something and its function. A physiologist would be the one studying, for example, how the cells of the heart communicate with one another in order to pump the blood.

LABRADOR

Animal behavior is the study of what animals do and why they do it. In this field, a behaviorist might study how animals communicate, why a spider eats her mate, or how a turtle can find the exact beach it was born on to lay its eggs.

LEAF

Botany is the study of plants- how they make food, grow, reproduce, and use energy. Since many of our foods and medicines come from plants, this is a pretty valuable field.

LEOPARD

Zoology is the study of animals. Jeff Corwin, Steve Irwin "Crocodile Hunter", and just about everyone you see on animal planet has a background in Zoology. This is a very broad field of study, and most people specialize within this field.

RESEARCHER

Biochemistry involves learning about the chemical reactions that make it possible for life to exist. You might find a biochemist studying a protein – how it’s made, where it is used in an organism, and why it is necessary for life.

Classifying and Naming Organisms

You are going to be looking at many living things within this course. We will begin by examining how biologists classify and name organisms. Just like the levels of organization, there is a hierarchy of classification which was created by Carl Linnaeus in the 1700s, and is still used today.
